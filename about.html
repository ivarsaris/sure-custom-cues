Philosophy

For me, a cue needs to provide a good hit and have low deflection characteristics. A quality construction, that's build to last, is obligatory. Players and cues are individual in so many aspects, so we need to be flexible when it comes to construction planning. To achieve the best match between the cue and the player, we work with different construction styles that are chosen to fit the player demands. We have a portfolio of shafts and shaft tapers, butt constructions, and other options, like cores, SureRESO handle etc. We combine the right elements so that every cue we make fits the player it goes to. Our aim is to provide the best possible cue in terms of playability and manufacturing quality.

My story

I started out as a snooker player. When I switched to play I couldn't find a right cue for me to play with, because I was used to my snooker cue. So, I started experimenting on my own at the age of 19 in 2005. At that time I felt that there were several characteristics missing in production cues. Over the years, I kept developing and looking for new elements to add to my cues. I have learned mostly from experimenting by myself. Today, I'm in contact with several cue makers and I think I found my own way of building a great player cue.
